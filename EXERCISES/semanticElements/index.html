Storj Decentralized Cloud Storage On A Blockchain

*---------------By Kashta Taylor Jan. 24,2018---------------*

Passionate about decentralization with a deep love of free and open-source software, Storj is on a mission to rethink cloud storage by providing the security, privacy, and transparency it's missing. Now building a platform that fundamentally changes the way people and devices own data.
As revealed exclusively to CoinDesk on December 20, 2017, Storj announced that it shall "lock-up" 245 million tokens, which are currently held in reserve, for another 6 months: valued then at over $300M. Of the 500M tokens created on the Ethereum platform per the project's May announcement, 70M were sold to the public. In this "unwieldy and opaque world of crypto-economics", some investors do not posess the desired level of clarity regarding the dates when a firm associated with a token's product development will have access to their much needed liquidity. As Mattew May, the CFO of Storj told CoinDesk: "Since these coin offerings are so new, there's a real need to be transparent. There's some market makers out there trying to manipulate these coins." Having all the funds they need for the next six months, by delaying the unlocking of the reserved tokens, the company is signaling to the community that they are stable and in it for the long haul and thus can get a better idea of how the cadence of the flow of token appears to be changing.
Storj will announce a detailed plan of their distribution plan for the tokens held in reserve, before the end of the new lock-up period. To help the company think through token economics in greater detail, several hires will be made over the next few months. As reported by CoinDesk, Storj's CTO Phillip Hutchins said, ""Generally, the consensus is they don't want to have large chunks of tokens looming out there without any idea of what's going to be done with them." Although the price of the tokens more than trippled in three months in 2017, delaying the release of the reserved tokens is a move to not affect the price of those coins which are already on the market. Quadrupling the ammount of total storage from 5 to 20 petabytes in 2 months at the end of 2017, thats not bad since one petabyte is equivalent to over 13 years of HD video. For more information regarding the 10m tokens sold during the token sale and where the 500m tokens in total are now, as mentioned by CoinDesk, refer to the Storj's token sale wrap-up blog post. Coindesk has also reported that Matthew May has concluded that: "Our ultimate goal and laser focus internally is to build a really beautiful, decent storage platform, and the interesting thing about the tokens is we are trying to make the best possible decisions about the token to create some stability to enable that platform." Well thank YOU CoinDesk and the good people at Storj for this good news. I'd tell you what I think personally, but I'm admitedly biased on the subject: I'm optimistic enough to HODL and aiming to HODL some more....I don't want to have to be catching rides from my friends in their Lambos, I'd rather drive into this beautiful future behind the wheel of my own Model S, maybe with a petabyte of storage for STORJ in the trunk...Free charging for life.....Hmmmmm?!!??
